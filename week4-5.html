    Week 4

    Week 4 was relatively slow in progress and I struggled to sift through ASL data. Many were outdated, lacked the information I needed, or had ambiguous glossing conventions.

    I am hoping to find data suitable for implementing a BERT model, since most successful WSD research uses BERT.

    As of now, I've explored:

    • ASL-Lex
    • ASL-Citizen
    • SignBank

    Most datasets seem to be optimized for sign language recognition, making this step rather difficult. I hope to find better data, or figure out how to combine different datasets to suit my needs.

    Week 5

    At this point, I realized I wanted to replicate previous WSD tasks. I was still struggling since I did not have English/ASL sentence pairs (That is, an English sentence with an ASL Gloss translation sentence)

    Thankfully, CV redirected me to ASLLRP Signbank, which provided me the sentence-pairs I was searching for. The website is incredibly confusing and slow, making my search quite frustrated. I was able to extract ~2000 pairs, but I am unsure if there are more I'm unaware of.

    I realized I should shift my focus to unsupervised training, since I am primarily relying on parallel language data. Instead of BERT (it failed), I hope to explore how Seq2Seq models function for English-ASL translation.

    For next week's presentation, I hope to show example of why I swapped BERT for Seq2Seq (essencially, why BERT failed). Then, I hope to figure out how I can reformat my research question to reflect my new WSD approach.
